Organizational

SRE Roles & Levels

L3 SRE (Junior) - Learning fundamentals, mentored projects, on-call shadow
L4 SRE (Mid-Level) - Independent projects, primary on-call, cross-team influence
L5 SRE (Senior) - Strategic initiatives, mentorship, architecture decisions
L6+ (Staff/Principal) - Company-wide reliability strategy, vision setting

Two Tracks:

  • IC Track (Individual Contributor) - Deep technical expertise, hands-on
  • Manager Track - People leadership, team building, career development
Culture

Production Mindset

Think Like a Reliability Engineer:

  • Failure Mode Analysis - "What could go wrong with this change?"
  • Blast Radius - Understanding scope of impact if something breaks
  • Defense in Depth - Multiple layers of protection, not single points of failure
  • Monitoring First - Can we observe this before deploying?
  • Rollback Capability - Always have an undo button
  • Graceful Degradation - System should degrade gracefully under load

The SRE Checklist:

  • Can we monitor this change?
  • Can we roll it back?
  • What's the worst that could happen?
Operations

On-Call Culture

  • Alert Fatigue - Too many alerts = ignored alerts. Monitor only what matters
  • Runbooks - Document every alert with troubleshooting steps
  • Escalation Paths - Clear who to contact at each level
  • Blameless Culture - Focus on systems, not people
  • Postmortems - Learn from every incident, document findings
  • On-Call Rotation - Fair schedules, time off to recover

Postmortem Format:

  • Timeline - when did what happen
  • Impact - what users/systems were affected
  • Root Cause - why it happened
  • Action Items - prevent recurrence
Roles

SRE vs Platform Engineering

SRE

  • Production reliability
  • On-call/incident response
  • System observability
  • Toil reduction
  • Operational best practices

Platform Engineering

  • Developer experience
  • Internal tools & platforms
  • Self-service infrastructure
  • CI/CD pipelines
  • Developer productivity

Overlap: Both focus on automation, observability, and reducing manual work.

Career

Career Path: Junior → Principal

L3 Junior SRE: Master Linux, basic cloud, learn one language (Python), shadow on-call
L4 SRE: Deep K8s/containers, observability stack, incident response, mentoring
L5 Senior SRE: Architecture decisions, cross-team projects, strategic reliability improvements
L6 Staff SRE: Company reliability strategy, major tech decisions, senior mentorship
L7+ Principal SRE: Industry leadership, long-term vision, organizational influence
Quiz

Knowledge Check

Q1: What is blast radius in the context of production mindset?
The scope of impact if something breaks - understanding how many users/systems are affected by a failure.
Q2: What is the primary goal of a blameless postmortem?
To learn from incidents and improve systems/processes, not to assign blame to individuals.
Q3: Difference between SRE and Platform Engineering?
SRE focuses on production reliability and on-call response. Platform Engineering focuses on developer experience and internal tools.
Q4: What does L5 SRE typically own?
Strategic initiatives, architecture decisions, cross-team influence, and mentorship of junior SREs.
Q5: Name 3 core skills for an SRE:
Any three from: Linux, networking, Kubernetes, observability, coding (Python/Go), cloud platforms (AWS/GCP/Azure).
