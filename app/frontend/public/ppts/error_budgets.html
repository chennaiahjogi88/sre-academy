What is an Error Budget?

Definition

The allowed amount of unreliability within a measurement window (month, quarter, year).

Error Budget = 100% - SLO
Example: If SLO = 99.9%, Error Budget = 0.1%

Concrete Example

SLO: 99.9% availability over one month

Budget = 0.1% of 30 days = 43.8 minutes

You can have 43.8 minutes of downtime in a month and still meet SLO.

Error Budget Math

SLO Target Monthly Budget Weekly Budget Daily Budget
99% 7.2 hours 1.68 hours 14.4 minutes
99.5% 3.6 hours 50 minutes 7.2 minutes
99.9% 43.8 minutes 10.1 minutes 1.44 minutes
99.95% 21.9 minutes 5 minutes 43.2 seconds
99.99% 4.4 minutes 1 minute 8.6 seconds

Key: Spent = total downtime in measurement window. Remaining = Budget - Spent

Error Budget Benefits

  • Shared Incentive Between Dev & SRE: Both teams benefit from shipping fast AND being reliable. Not a conflict anymore.
  • Data-Driven Reliability Decisions: "Can we ship this feature?" Answer: Check the budget. Objective, not emotional.
  • Innovation Acceleration: If you have budget remaining, you can take calculated risks. Deploy faster, do more experiments.
  • Justifies Investment: When budget is exhausted, it's data-backed rationale to stop feature work and focus on reliability.

Error Budget Policy

Typical Policy Structure

  • When budget > 50%: Business as usual. Ship features normally.
  • When budget 25-50%: Yellow zone. Code review tightens. Risky features blocked.
  • When budget < 25%: Red zone. Feature freeze. All hands on reliability.
  • When budget exhausted (0%): Stop new feature releases. Focus 100% on stability. Incident review required.

Written Policy Document Should Include

  • Clear SLO targets per service
  • Measurement window (rolling 30 days, calendar month, etc.)
  • Decision thresholds
  • Approval process (who decides when to stop shipping?)

Error Budget Tracking

What to Track

  • Budget Burned Over Time: Show as percentage burned daily/weekly. When will it hit 100%?
  • Burn Rate: How fast are you consuming budget? (Introduced in next module)
  • Alerts: Alert when > 50% burned in first week of month. This is unsustainable pace.
  • Service Comparison: Which services burn fastest? Invest in reliability for high-burners.

Dashboard Should Show

  • Budget consumed (in minutes or percentage)
  • Budget remaining
  • Days until 100% consumed (if current pace continues)
  • Trend: Is burn rate improving or worsening?

PromQL for Error Budget Calculation

Calculate Remaining Monthly Error Budget

# SLO target = 99.9% slo_target := 0.999; # Current availability SLI over full month (30 days) current_availability := sum(rate(requests_total{status=~"2.."}[30d])) / sum(rate(requests_total[30d])); # Error budget remaining (as percentage) error_budget_remaining := (current_availability - slo_target) / (1 - slo_target) * 100

Alert When Budget Consumed > 50% in First Week

burned_this_week := ((0.999 - current_availability[7d]) / (1 - 0.999)); alert EarlyBudgetBurn : burned_this_week > 0.5

Quiz: Error Budgets

Q1: If your SLO is 99.9%, what percentage of time can you be unavailable?
A: 0.1% (100% - 99.9%). This is your error budget.
Q2: You have a 99% SLO over a month (30 days). How many minutes of downtime can you have?
A: 7.2 hours = 432 minutes. Calculation: 30 days × 24 hours × 60 min × 1% = 432 minutes
Q3: What are three benefits of an error budget framework?
A: (1) Shared incentive between Dev/SRE, (2) Data-driven reliability decisions, (3) Justifies reliability investment
Q4: When budget is exhausted (0% remaining), what should happen?
A: Stop new feature releases, focus 100% on stability, conduct incident review to understand root causes of budget burn
Q5: You burned 75% of your monthly budget in the first week. Why is this a problem?
A: At this burn rate, you'll exhaust the entire budget in 1.3 weeks, forcing a feature freeze for the rest of the month. Unsustainable pace.
